## Thread

### 1/7

Everyone wants to build the token launch. Almost nobody wants to operate the next 36 months: investor cliffs, contributor pay, treasury outflows, staking rewards, and airdrop claims—without one bad spreadsheet cell becoming an onchain mistake. 🧵

### 2/7

After reading through Streamflow Business, the cleanest description is: the back office after launch.

@streamflow_fi puts vesting, locks, staking, airdrops, payouts, treasury, and branded claim portals in one non-custodial Solana stack.

### 3/7

Picture the ops flow:

upload recipients → choose an unlock schedule → fund once → let contributors claim from your branded portal → watch every unlock onchain.

The contracts execute the rules; your team keeps control of the tokens.

### 4/7

Who is it for?

• a protocol founder before TGE  
• an ops lead paying a distributed team  
• a foundation running grants  
• a DAO managing recurring distributions

If token ops involve calendars, CSVs, or custom scripts, you’ve outgrown a point tool.

### 5/7

Why not build it yourself?

Because the hard part isn’t a vesting form. It’s audited execution, migrations, fee handling, recipient UX, tracking, and scale.

Streamflow says 40K+ projects use its rails, with airdrops supporting up to 1M recipients.

### 6/7

The math is unusually legible:

Starter begins at $199/mo. Business at $499/mo adds white-glove onboarding, 50 vesting contracts, up to 10 locks, 3 staking pools, 2 airdrops, a token dashboard, and a white-label portal.

### 7/7

Solana teams should spend founder time on distribution, not rebuilding token infrastructure.

If your token has employees, investors, contributors, or a community, give it a real finance stack—not a folder of scripts.
